MVP


THE LTBO MVP IS ANNOUNCED!

Most Valuable Player

After a terrific 5 weeks of basketball, full with skill, highlight moves, and incredible looks, the Lunch Time Basketball Organisation has released it's MVP, voted by the players. Votes were awarded as 3 points for first, 2 points for second, and 1 point for third. With 94 points up for grabs, the three young men below each combined for seventy-three of them, making them the clear favourites. So, the final standings of the MVP from Season 1 of the Lunch Time Basketball Organisation is...




3rd Place | Michael Iffland

Michael Iffland

Team: The 5 Musketeers | Votes: 20


With 20 votes, star passer Michael Iffland earns himself third place on the MVP voting list. With highlight passes, tough shots, and good laughs, there's no surprise he found himself on this list. Averaging a solid 1.5 points, as well as winning the 3rd man award means Michael has had an incredibly solid season. With their team averaging 6 points a game, it wouldn't be a surprise if he got an assist on 3 of those each break. His passing is comparable to Jokic's, and, if that means anything, he might find himself MVP next season.

2nd Place | Lukas Johnston

Lukas Johnston

Team: The 5 Musketeers | Votes: 24


Coming in with 24 votes, athletic teammate of Michael, Lukas Johnston takes home second place! It's quite incredible that two of the top 3 MVP rankings go to one team, highlighting just how much talent the 5 Musketeers have. Lukas has been known all season for his springiness, jaw-dropping athleticism, and unreal acrobatic finishes that somehow manage to go in. Lukas leads his team in scoring, averaging two points per game (landing him a spot on the all-offensive team). But it doesn't stop there - he's quite the menace on defence, able to guard any guard and forward in the league, getting countless intercepts and rebounds. While they're not common, he has his share of highlight blocks, flying through the air and throwing it away. It's hard to believe that this guy didn't get MVP.
1st Place | William Kim

William Kim

Team: The Loose Gooses | Votes: 29


WILLIAM KIM WINS MVP! Living up the hype of being the Number 1 pick in the draft, Loose Gooses' forward William Kim has won the first ever LTBO MVP. Averaging a solid 1.7 points per game, and a career high of six points, William Kim is a straight scorer. Known for his signature turn-around fade in the post, his bag is LOADED with moves, from step backs, to tough layups, and even a beautiful three point shot. He's certainly one of the most talented scorers in the league, backed up by his spot on the all-offensive team. While he's known for his offence, you still wouldn't want him guarding you; this man not only came second for the perimeter defender award, but was on the all-defensive team as well. He can literally levitate - unless you're lucky he's sending your shot to the opposite court. With a heap of steals and being one of the best rebounders in the league, this man, quite literally, does it all. Unless he rejects the position, it wouldn't be surprising if we see him step up as GM next season to replace Rudy Hoschke. And finally, how could we forget, but the man is sexy AF. How could you NOT expect this guy to win?



One final big congratulation to the top three, and, of course, an even special congraulation to William Kim for coming home with the trophy. With just two days left in the tournament, you three are needed more than ever by your team to get you wins. The LTBO wishes good luck, it'll be one hell of a finish.
