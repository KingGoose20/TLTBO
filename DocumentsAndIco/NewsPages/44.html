Willie's Play of the Day | Finals

Nick's and-1 that sent Lukas tumbling is the play of the finals!

A small disclaimer before I commence the first Willie Play of the Day for the finals: The beef has officially ended between Munkee’s Magic Moment and Willie’s Play of the Day!!!!! Michael and I have come to the conclusion that this town is in fact big enough for the two of us and have agreed to stop hacking away at each other. I hope this is for the best and wish Michael the best in his endevours. Also I am thinking of posting a survey at the end of the season on which of the 5 (or 6 by then) Willie POTDs was the best so I know how to improve for next season. Now let us commence the WilliE POTD for day 1 of the finals:
FA2: WW POTD F1, by Will Weekes



Rationale:

The research question for this report goes as follows: “Was Nick Szogi’s play against Lukas was gnarly enough to be POTD for finals day 1?” The play went as shown in Figure 1 where Lukas Johnston of the 5 Musketeers went straight over the top of Nick Szogi of the Wet Willies while attempting to steal the ball. This report will show evidence from a variety of different sources to support or refute the research question.





Analysis of Evidence:

There are several primary sources of evidence available to evaluate whether the research question is proven. One of these is Jasper Collier’s account of the events that went as follows, “Yeah bruv it was totally sick as ay brah, it was just so Phat like Lukas just went flying over him ay. Far out brah yeeeeew” (Collier, 2023). This shows that Nick’s play was indeed radical and worthy of POTD. Furthermore, William Kim of the Loose Gooses stated after a short interview that “Yeah I had to clean my pants out after that one, if that wasn’t a POTD then I don’t know what is!” This further shows that Nicks play was good.



Conclusion:

In Conclusion, It has been decided that Nicks sick play against Lukas should be POTD because people reckon it is pretty mint. Congrats Nick and cya all next time, Willie out.

- Willie Weekes, forward of the Wet Willies
