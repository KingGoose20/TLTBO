Crunching Numbers | Term 2


TLTBO CEO Angus Walker is back with the first 'Crunching Numbers' of the second season of lunchtime basketball.

Overview

Welcome back to Crunching Numbers, the first article from this season! In this article, I’m going to break down some interesting stats throughout the league. I’ve broken down three stats into detail, and at the bottom of this page will be a list of some other statistics. Use this information as you will, statistic analysis can be the difference between a winning and losing team.



1. The 5 Musketeers are the only team that has a declining win %

As the heading says, the 5 Musketeers are the only team in the league that is trending in a losing direction. After starting their first 4 days winning 65% of games, they have broken 50% just once, and have been at just 31% since. Their most recent day they were 1-10, a crushing 9%. While this is most likely due to being incredibly undermanned, and missing their center piece Alexander Galt, these recent struggles are certainly a worry for the Musketeers, as they’ve seen their 5 point lead on second dwindle to a 4 point lead on third place. With Galt returning throughout this week, will he help bring the 5 Musketeers over their slump, back to winning basketball? On the other hand, the Wet Willies have had the biggest spike in performance, increasing from 37% over the first four days to 66% since then. They are now sitting at 56%, the only team above 50. Despite this, the WW still sit in third, but they can’t be overlooked; they’re creeping up on not just second, but first place.



2. Total games played increase

Another big change from last season is that the average number of games played each day has increased from 15 to 20 (a 33% increase). This change not just shows that we’re getting more action, but the talent in the league has improved. Remember, each playing session is the same length, so the only reason for these changes is that the scoring ability has improved. Big congratulations to the league for such a massive improvement.



3. Lopsided dominance

After some detailed analysis, I’ve found some interesting trends in the data. Certain players seem to play better against certain teams, and significantly worse against others. Alexander Galt of the 5 Musketeers scores 2 of his 2.6 ppg against the Loose Gooses, or ~80% of his total points. On the other hand, Samuel McConaghy scores just 25% of his points against the Loose Gooses, or just 0.6 of his 2.4 average. On the other hand, Angus Walker scores 64% of his points against the Wet Willies, and Clarrie Jones scores 67% against the 5 Musketeers. Michael Iffland and Ryan Pattemore are split right down the middle, scoring 50% of their points against each team.
