Preseason Summary | 20/04

EX-GM Rudy Hoschke has provided a detailed preseason summary!

Read below

Well, what a preseason there has been with some very amazing plays, but let’s take a look into the teams; The Wet Willes and their big three are just warming up, the Loose Gooses and their rocky start to preseason are looking to get back on track, and the 5 Musketeers and how the big two of Sam and Alex dominate the games with the unstoppable force. First, let's dive deeper into the CEO minds of what preseason is. Preseason is a very special period of preparation before the upcoming regular season. It is a time when teams get back in shape, develop strategies and work on their game plans. It helps players get into shape and prepare for the upcoming regular season (Lukas).

Preseason games are important because they provide everyone with the opportunity to experiment with different line-ups and tactics, while also evaluating the strengths and weaknesses of their players. This is especially true for new players or rookies, who are trying to impress coaches and earn a spot on the team's roster.

Overall, preseason in sports is an important time for teams and players to prepare for the upcoming regular season, and it provides fans with a glimpse of what's to come. While the results of preseason games do not count towards the regular season standings, they are still valuable opportunities for teams and players to improve and get ready for the challenges ahead.

Well at the conclusion of the preseason it is clear that there is a favourite team to win, throughout the week they have shown their clear dominance in their two-man game, being unstoppable from mid-range and finishing, while also dominating on the boards. Alex draws the defenders in which leads to plenty of space for Samuel McConaghy to shoot the ball effectively. While it is also to see sitting on the side lines, they have a strong two-man game but doesn’t really pass to their teammates.

While also the sharp shooting of another team has been shown through the final couple of days of the preseason. With the first 3’s made and then to follow it up quickly by another one the GM selected these players who space the floor and effectively shoot the ball this leaves a lot of space for their star finishing Angus Walker. While Will Kim also spreads the floor, he is one of the defensive strong holds for this team with his awe inspiring blocks.

And Finally, onto the last team the Wet Willes they have the “Big three” while it takes a while for the Michael and Lukas to warm up from their holidays they are going to need to step up to lead their team scoring less than 2 in total over the week. While this team doesn’t look the best on paper they come through in the game with the flashy passes of Michael Iffland while also the acrobatic leaping of Lukas Johnston so hopefully they step up before the start of the regular season.

- Rudy Hoschke, center of the Wet Willies
